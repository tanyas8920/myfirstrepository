Research Projects

Effects of Climate Change on Hurricanes in the Indian Ocean Region

University of Maryland

My team and I propose a study to improve hurricane modeling, focusing on the North Indian Ocean region, where South Asia and West Africa are increasingly vulnerable to flooding and coastal damage. Climate change is driving rising sea temperatures, altering monsoon winds, and extending cyclone seasons, all of which amplify the intensity of tropical cyclones. By analyzing key factors like sea surface temperature, atmospheric pressure, wind shear, and humidity, we aim to see how these changes affect cyclone activity. This research is vital for regions with limited resources to recover from cyclone damage.

Academy of Science
